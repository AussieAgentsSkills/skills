# Australian Consumer Law (ACL)

You are an expert in Australian Consumer Law, consumer guarantees, and business compliance.

## Consumer Guarantees

Products MUST be:
1. **Of acceptable quality** - safe, durable, no defects
2. **Fit for purpose** - do what they're meant to
3. **Match description** - match what was advertised
4. **Match sample** - same as any demo/sample
5. **Have spare parts available** - for reasonable time

Services MUST be:
1. **Provided with due care and skill**
2. **Fit for specified purpose**
3. **Completed in reasonable time** (if no deadline set)

## Refund Rights

### Major Problem - Consumer Choice
Consumer can choose:
- Full refund
- Replacement
- Compensation for drop in value

### Minor Problem - Business Choice
Business can choose:
- Repair in reasonable time
- Replacement
- Refund

### What's a Major Problem?
- Product wouldn't have been purchased if problem known
- Significantly different from description/sample
- Substantially unfit for normal purpose
- Unsafe

## No Refund Signs = Illegal

Businesses CANNOT say:
- "No refunds"
- "No refunds on sale items"
- "Exchange only"
- "No refunds after 30 days"

These signs violate ACL (fines up to $50,000 for companies).

## When Refunds Don't Apply

- Change of mind (unless store policy allows)
- Knew about the fault before buying
- Damaged by misuse
- Got what was asked for, just didn't like it

## Warranties

### Consumer Guarantees vs Manufacturer Warranty
| Consumer Guarantee | Manufacturer Warranty |
|--------------------|----------------------|
| Automatic by law | Voluntary extra |
| Against retailer | Against manufacturer |
| Can't be excluded | Terms can vary |
| Based on product type/price | Time-limited |

### Extended Warranties
- Often duplicate consumer rights
- Must provide value beyond ACL
- Retailer must explain ACL rights when selling

## Misleading Conduct

Businesses must NOT:
- Make false claims about products
- Create false impressions
- Omit important information
- Use bait advertising
- Make false testimonials

## Unfair Contract Terms

For standard form contracts, terms are unfair if they:
- Cause significant imbalance
- Aren't reasonably necessary to protect business
- Would cause detriment to consumer

Unfair terms = void (but rest of contract stands).

## Product Safety

Mandatory safety standards for:
- Baby walkers
- Cots
- Bicycles
- Sunglasses
- Many more products

Report unsafe products to: productsafety.gov.au

## Small Business Protection

ACL also covers business-to-business transactions where:
- Goods/services ≤$100,000
- Goods for personal/domestic use
- Vehicle or trailer for transport

## Enforcement

| Body | Role |
|------|------|
| ACCC | National enforcement, systemic issues |
| State/Territory Fair Trading | Local complaints, individual disputes |
| AFCA | Financial services complaints |

## Penalties

| Contravention | Maximum Penalty |
|---------------|-----------------|
| False/misleading conduct | $50M (company) |
| Unfair contract terms | $50M (company) |
| Consumer guarantee breach | Compensation + penalties |
