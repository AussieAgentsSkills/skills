# Victorian Rental Laws

You are an expert in Victorian residential tenancy law under the Residential Tenancies Act 1997.

## Rental Increases (2024 Rules)

- **Maximum one increase per 12 months**
- **60 days written notice required**
- Tenant can challenge "excessive" increases at VCAT
- No increase during fixed term unless lease allows it

## Bond

### Amount
- Maximum 4 weeks rent (most properties)
- Maximum 1 month rent if weekly rent >$900

### Lodgement
- Must lodge with RTBA within 10 business days
- Tenant receives receipt
- Bond held by government, not landlord

### Return
- Claim within 10 business days of lease end
- Both parties sign bond claim form
- Disputes go to VCAT

## Notice Periods

### Tenant Ending Lease
| Situation | Notice |
|-----------|--------|
| End of fixed term | 28 days |
| Periodic (month-to-month) | 28 days |
| Breaking fixed term | 28 days + break fee may apply |

### Landlord Ending Lease
| Reason | Notice |
|--------|--------|
| End of fixed term | 90 days |
| Sale of property | 60 days |
| Landlord moving in | 60 days |
| Breach (rent arrears 14+ days) | 14 days |
| No reason (periodic) | 90 days |

## Minimum Standards (since Mar 2021)

All rentals MUST have:
- Working toilet, bathroom, kitchen
- Hot and cold water
- Weatherproof roof and walls
- External window coverings (bedroom)
- Functioning stove/cooktop
- Heating in main living area
- Working locks on external doors
- Adequate ventilation
- Electrical safety switch

## Urgent Repairs

Landlord must fix within 24 hours:
- Burst water pipes
- Gas leak
- Dangerous electrical fault
- Flooding
- Serious roof leak
- No hot water (in winter)
- Blocked toilet (only toilet)
- Serious safety issue

Tenant can arrange if landlord doesn't respond:
- Up to $2,500 without prior approval
- Must make reasonable attempts to contact landlord

## Rent Arrears

| Days Behind | What Happens |
|-------------|--------------|
| 1-13 days | Landlord can send reminder |
| 14+ days | Landlord can issue breach notice |
| 14 days after breach notice | Can apply to VCAT for possession |

## Inspections

- Maximum 4 routine inspections per year
- 7 days written notice required
- Must be at reasonable time
- Tenant can negotiate time

## Pets

- Landlords cannot unreasonably refuse pets
- Can refuse if property unsuitable (no fence, body corp rules)
- Can require professional cleaning at end of tenancy

## Modifications

Tenant can make minor modifications without consent:
- Picture hooks (reasonable number)
- Wall anchors for safety (furniture/TV)
- Wireless security cameras (no damage)

## VCAT

Victorian Civil and Administrative Tribunal handles disputes:
- Bond claims
- Repairs disputes
- Rent increase challenges
- Compensation claims
- Eviction applications

Application fee: ~$65 (tenant), ~$70-300 (landlord)
