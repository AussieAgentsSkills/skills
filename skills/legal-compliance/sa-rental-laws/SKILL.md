# SA Rental Laws

You are an expert in SA residential tenancy law under the Residential Tenancies Act 1995.

## Bond

### Amount
- Maximum 4 weeks rent (unfurnished)
- Maximum 6 weeks rent (furnished)

### Lodgement
- Landlord must lodge with CBS within 2 weeks
- Tenant receives receipt

### Return
- Apply online via CBS
- Both parties must agree OR
- SACAT if disputed

## Rent Increases

### Frequency
- Fixed term: Only if lease allows
- Periodic: Maximum once per 12 months

### Notice
- 60 days written notice
- Can apply to SACAT if excessive

## Notice Periods

### Tenant Ending Lease
| Situation | Notice |
|-----------|--------|
| End of fixed term | 28 days |
| Periodic lease | 21 days |
| Breaking fixed term | Negotiated + costs |

### Landlord Ending Lease
| Reason | Notice |
|--------|--------|
| No grounds (end of fixed) | 28 days |
| No grounds (periodic) | 90 days |
| Sale of property | 60 days |
| Breach | 14 days |
| Non-payment (14+ days arrears) | 7 days |

## Repairs

### Urgent Repairs
- Essential services failure
- Serious safety hazard
- Landlord must respond within 24-48 hours

### Non-Urgent Repairs
- Written request to landlord
- Reasonable time to complete
- Can apply to SACAT if not fixed

## Inspections

### Routine
- Maximum 4 per year
- 7-14 days written notice
- Reasonable time of day
- Tenant may be present

## Pets

- Must apply in writing
- Landlord has 14 days to respond
- Cannot unreasonably refuse
- Can require conditions

## Breaking a Lease

Costs may include:
- Re-letting fee (1-2 weeks rent)
- Advertising costs
- Lost rent until new tenant

## SACAT (Tribunal)

SA Civil and Administrative Tribunal handles:
- Bond disputes
- Repairs orders
- Rent disputes
- Termination

Application fee: ~$75-100
