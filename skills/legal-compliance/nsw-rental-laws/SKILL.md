# NSW Rental Laws

You are an expert in NSW residential tenancy law under the Residential Tenancies Act 2010.

## Bond

### Amount
- Maximum 4 weeks rent

### Lodgement
- Landlord must lodge with NSW Fair Trading within 10 days
- Tenant receives receipt via Rental Bonds Online

### Return
- Apply via Rental Bonds Online
- Both parties must agree OR
- NCAT if disputed

## Rent Increases

### Frequency
- Fixed term: Only if lease allows
- Periodic: Maximum once per 12 months

### Notice
- 60 days written notice
- Tenant can apply to NCAT if excessive

## Notice Periods

### Tenant Ending Lease
| Situation | Notice |
|-----------|--------|
| End of fixed term | 14 days |
| During fixed term (with break fee) | 14 days |
| Periodic lease | 21 days |

### Landlord Ending Lease
| Reason | Notice |
|--------|--------|
| No grounds (end of fixed term) | 30 days |
| No grounds (periodic) | 90 days |
| Sale of property | 30 days |
| Major renovations | 60 days |
| Breach | 14 days |
| Non-payment (14+ days arrears) | 14 days |

**Note:** No-grounds evictions being phased out from 2025

## Repairs

### Urgent Repairs (within 24-48 hours)
- Burst water service
- Blocked toilet
- Serious roof leak
- Gas leak
- Dangerous electrical fault
- Flooding
- Serious storm damage

### Non-Urgent Repairs
- Landlord has 14 days to respond to written request
- If no response, tenant can:
  - Apply to NCAT
  - Arrange repairs up to $1,000 (with conditions)

## Minimum Standards (from 2025)

All rentals must have:
- Working bathroom, kitchen, laundry
- Hot and cold water
- Lighting in each room
- Window coverings in bedrooms
- Adequate ventilation
- Structurally sound

## Inspections

### Routine
- Maximum 4 per year
- 7 days written notice
- Reasonable time of day
- Tenant can refuse unreasonable times

### Entry Without Notice
Only if:
- Emergency
- Tenant agrees at the time

## Pets

- Landlord cannot unreasonably refuse
- Can refuse if:
  - Strata prohibits
  - Property unsuitable
- Can require additional cleaning clause

## Breaking a Lease

### Break Fees
| Lease remaining | Fee |
|-----------------|-----|
| 25%+ of term | 4 weeks rent |
| <25% of term | 3 weeks rent |
| <10% of term | 2 weeks rent |

Plus: Re-letting costs, advertising (reasonable)

## Water Charges

Tenant pays usage if:
- Property has individual meter
- Separately metered
- Water efficiency measures installed

## Subletting

- Need landlord's written consent
- Cannot unreasonably refuse
- Original tenant remains liable

## NCAT (Tribunal)

NSW Civil and Administrative Tribunal handles:
- Bond disputes
- Repairs orders
- Rent disputes
- Termination orders

Application fee: ~$55 (tenant), ~$130 (landlord)
