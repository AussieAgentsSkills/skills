# Australian Import/Export

You are an expert in Australian import and export regulations.

## Importing Goods

### GST on Imports
- 10% GST applies to most imported goods
- Collected by Australian Border Force
- Can claim back if GST registered

### GST-Free Threshold
- Goods valued ≤$1,000 AUD: No GST at border
- Exception: Tobacco, alcohol always taxed

### Customs Duty
Varies by:
- Type of goods (tariff classification)
- Country of origin (Free Trade Agreements)
- Value of goods

### Import Process
1. **Order goods** from overseas supplier
2. **Goods arrive** at Australian port/airport
3. **Customs clearance** (broker or self)
4. **Pay duties/GST** if applicable
5. **Goods released** for delivery

### When to Use Customs Broker
- Commercial shipments
- Restricted goods
- High-value shipments
- Complex tariff classification

## Exporting Goods

### GST on Exports
- **GST-free** if exported
- Can claim GST credits on inputs
- Must have export evidence

### Export Evidence Required
- Bill of lading or airway bill
- Export declaration
- Commercial invoice
- Proof of export within 60 days

### Export Permits
Some goods need permits:
- Defence items
- Cultural heritage
- Endangered species (CITES)
- Dual-use goods

## Tariff Classification

Uses Harmonised System (HS) codes:
- 8-digit code for classification
- Determines duty rate
- Search via: harmonizedcode.com

### Common Duty Rates
| Product | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Clothing | 5% |
| Electronics | 0-5% |
| Cars | 5% |
| Food | 0-5% |

## Free Trade Agreements (FTAs)

Reduced/zero duty from FTA countries:
- China (ChAFTA)
- USA (AUSFTA)
- Japan (JAEPA)
- Korea (KAFTA)
- ASEAN (AANZFTA)
- UK (A-UKFTA)
- NZ (ANZCERTA)

### Certificate of Origin
Required to claim FTA rate. Forms vary by agreement.

## Prohibited & Restricted Imports

### Prohibited
- Drugs
- Weapons (most)
- Counterfeit goods
- Child exploitation material

### Restricted (need permit)
- Medicines
- Food
- Animals & plants
- Firearms
- Heritage items

## Biosecurity

Australia has strict biosecurity:
- Declare all food, plant, animal products
- Penalties up to $660,000 / 10 years
- Check BICON: bicon.agriculture.gov.au

## Import Calculations

### Example: $5,000 goods from non-FTA country
```
Goods value (FOB): $5,000
Freight & insurance: $500
Customs value (CIF): $5,500
Duty @ 5%: $275
GST @ 10% on ($5,500 + $275): $577.50
Total to pay: $852.50
```

## Integrated Cargo System (ICS)

Electronic system for customs:
- Import declarations
- Export declarations
- Cargo reports

Access via licensed broker or self-service.

## Record Keeping

Keep for 5 years:
- Commercial invoices
- Customs entries
- Import/export permits
- Proof of export
- Certificate of origin
