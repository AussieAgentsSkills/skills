# Annual Leave

Paid time off for rest and recreation. A National Employment Standard (NES) entitlement.

## The Entitlement

- **Full-time:** 4 weeks (152 hours) per year
- **Part-time:** 4 weeks pro-rata based on hours
- **Casual:** None (compensated by [[wages/casual-loading|25% loading]])
- **Shift workers:** May get 5 weeks (check [[MOCs/awards|award]])

## How It Accrues

Leave accrues progressively from day one:
- 4 weeks ÷ 52 weeks = 0.077 weeks per week worked
- Or: 152 hours ÷ 52 = 2.92 hours per week

Accrues during:
- Paid leave
- Public holidays
- Community service leave

Does NOT accrue during:
- Unpaid leave
- Unpaid parental leave
- Workers comp (in most cases)

## Taking Leave

1. Employee requests leave
2. Employer must not unreasonably refuse
3. Leave is taken at a time agreed between both

**Employer can direct leave:**
- During shutdown periods (e.g., Christmas)
- If employee has excessive accrual (8+ weeks)
- Must give reasonable notice

**Employee can't be forced to take leave** that would reduce balance below the 4-week annual entitlement (except shutdowns).

## Leave Loading

Some awards and agreements include 17.5% annual leave loading:
- Extra payment when taking annual leave
- Check your [[MOCs/awards|award]]

## Payment

Annual leave is paid at the employee's base rate of pay:
- What they would have earned if working
- Includes regular allowances and loadings
- Does NOT include overtime

## Cashing Out

Annual leave can be cashed out if:
1. Award or agreement permits
2. Written agreement each time
3. Employee keeps minimum 4 weeks accrued
4. Paid at full rate (including loading if applicable)

## On Termination

Accrued annual leave is paid out on [[termination/final-pay|termination]]:
- At the base rate
- Including leave loading if applicable
- Regardless of how employment ends
