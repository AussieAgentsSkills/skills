# Public Holidays

Employees get a paid day off on public holidays (or penalty rates if they work).

## National Public Holidays

| Holiday | Date |
|---------|------|
| New Year's Day | 1 January |
| Australia Day | 26 January |
| Good Friday | Varies (March/April) |
| Easter Saturday | Day after Good Friday |
| Easter Monday | Day after Easter Sunday |
| Anzac Day | 25 April |
| Queen's Birthday | Varies by state (June/Sept) |
| Christmas Day | 25 December |
| Boxing Day | 26 December |

## State/Territory Holidays

Additional holidays vary by state:
- **VIC:** Melbourne Cup Day (metro), AFL Grand Final Friday
- **QLD:** Royal Queensland Show (Brisbane area)
- **SA:** Adelaide Cup Day, Proclamation Day
- **WA:** Western Australia Day
- **TAS:** Royal Hobart Regatta, Recreation Day (north)
- **ACT:** Canberra Day, Family & Community Day, Reconciliation Day
- **NT:** May Day, Picnic Day

## Substitute Days

When a public holiday falls on a weekend:
- Saturday holiday → usually observed Monday
- Sunday holiday → usually observed Monday (or Tuesday if Monday is also a holiday)

Check your state's gazette for official substitute days.

## Working on Public Holidays

Employees can be asked to work if request is reasonable. Can refuse if:
- Personal circumstances (family, caring, religious)
- Refusal is reasonable
- Request is unreasonable (short notice, nature of work)

If working: [[wages/penalty-rates|penalty rates apply]] — usually 250%+.

## Payment for Not Working

If you don't work the public holiday, you get paid:
- Your ordinary hours for that day
- As if you had worked a normal day

Casuals only get paid if they work the day.

## Part-Time Employees

Only get the benefit if the holiday falls on a day they would normally work.
