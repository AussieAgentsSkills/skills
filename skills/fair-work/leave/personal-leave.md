# Personal/Carer's Leave

Paid leave for when you're sick or need to care for a sick family member.

## The Entitlement

- **Full-time:** 10 days per year
- **Part-time:** 10 days pro-rata based on hours
- **Casual:** Unpaid carer's leave only (2 days per occasion)

## What It's For

### Personal (Sick) Leave
- You're unfit for work due to illness or injury
- Includes mental health

### Carer's Leave
- A member of your immediate family or household needs care
- Due to illness, injury, or unexpected emergency

**Immediate family:** Spouse, child, parent, grandparent, grandchild, sibling (including step/in-law).

## Accumulation

- Accrues from day one
- Carries over year to year
- **Does not expire** while employed
- Long-term employees can have large balances

## NOT Paid Out

Unlike annual leave, personal leave is **not paid out on termination**.

"Use it or lose it" when employment ends.

## Taking Leave

1. Notify employer as soon as practical
2. State expected duration
3. Provide evidence if requested

## Evidence

Employer can request evidence for:
- Single day absences
- Absences before/after weekends or public holidays
- Pattern of absences

Acceptable evidence:
- Medical certificate
- Statutory declaration
- Pharmacist certificate (some awards)

Employer must pay for the certificate if they require one for absence <3 days (in some states/awards).

## Abuse of Leave

Taking personal leave when not genuinely sick/caring is misconduct. Can lead to:
- Disciplinary action
- Dismissal (if serious/repeated)
