# Employee vs Contractor Test

The law looks at the **real nature of the relationship**, not what the contract says.

## The Multi-Factor Test

Courts consider the totality of the relationship. No single factor is decisive.

### Factors Pointing to EMPLOYEE

| Factor | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Control over how work is done | Employer directs the method, not just the outcome |
| Set hours/roster | Employee works when told |
| Uses employer's tools/equipment | Doesn't need own investment |
| Paid hourly/weekly | Not per job/outcome |
| Can't subcontract | Must do work personally |
| Works mainly for one business | Financially dependent |
| Integrated into business | Part of the team, uses business email, attends meetings |
| Training provided | Employer invests in their skills |
| Uniform/branding | Represents the business |
| Ongoing expectation of work | Not project-by-project |

### Factors Pointing to CONTRACTOR

| Factor | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Controls how work is done | Decides own methods |
| Sets own hours | Works when they choose |
| Uses own tools/equipment | Has made capital investment |
| Quotes per job | Invoices for completed work |
| Can subcontract | Can send someone else |
| Multiple clients | Not dependent on one |
| Operates own business | Has ABN, insurance, business structure |
| Risk of profit/loss | Bears commercial risk |
| Provides own training | Maintains own skills |
| Project-based work | Clear start and end |

## The Modern Test (High Court 2022)

The High Court clarified in *CFMMEU v Personnel Contracting* and *ZG Operations v Jamsek*:

1. **Start with the contract** — what does it say?
2. **But the contract must reflect reality** — if the terms don't match what actually happens, real practice wins
3. **Look at the totality** — no single factor is decisive

## Red Flags for Sham Contracting

These patterns suggest someone is really an employee:

- ❌ Existing employee becomes "contractor" doing same job
- ❌ Only work for your business
- ❌ You set their hours and they can't refuse
- ❌ Must do work personally, can't send a substitute
- ❌ You provide all equipment
- ❌ ABN was condition of "employment"
- ❌ Paid regularly like wages, not per invoice

## Consequences of Getting It Wrong

If they're really an employee:
- Back-pay for award entitlements
- Unpaid [[wages/superannuation]] + interest + penalties
- Unpaid [[leave/annual-leave]] and [[leave/personal-leave]]
- [[termination/unfair-dismissal]] claim if terminated
- Sham contracting penalties: up to $93,900 (individual) / $469,500 (company)
