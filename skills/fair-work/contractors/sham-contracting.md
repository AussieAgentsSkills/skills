# Sham Contracting

Calling someone a contractor when they're really an employee is illegal.

## What Is Sham Contracting?

Three prohibited actions under the Fair Work Act:

1. **Misrepresenting employment as contracting**
   - Telling an employee they're a contractor
   - When the relationship is really employment

2. **Dismissing to re-engage as contractor**
   - Firing an employee
   - Then hiring them back as a "contractor"
   - To do the same or similar work

3. **Making false statements about contractor status**
   - Telling someone they're a contractor
   - To avoid paying entitlements

## Penalties

### Per Breach

| Who | Maximum Penalty |
|-----|-----------------|
| Individual | $93,900 |
| Company | $469,500 |

**Plus:**
- Back-pay of all entitlements
- Superannuation + interest
- Prosecution costs
- Reputational damage

### Serious Contraventions

Where conduct is deliberate and part of a systematic pattern:
- Penalties can be **10x higher**
- Up to $4.69 million per breach for companies

## The "Recklessness" Defence

An employer can avoid penalties if they prove they:
- Did NOT know the person was an employee
- Were NOT reckless about whether they were

But ignorance is hard to claim when:
- The relationship was obviously employment
- They didn't seek advice
- Industry practice is employment

## Common Sham Arrangements

### The ABN Requirement
"You need an ABN to work here" — if they'd otherwise be an employee, this is sham contracting.

### The Redundancy-to-Contractor Trick
Employee made redundant Monday, starts as "contractor" Tuesday doing same work. Clear sham.

### The "We Only Use Contractors" Policy
Industry doesn't matter — if the relationship is employment, they're employees.

### The Gig Economy Grey Area
Some gig platforms have been found to employ workers despite "contractor" labels:
- Deliveroo riders
- Some Uber drivers
- Food delivery workers

## Enforcement

Fair Work Ombudsman actively investigates sham contracting:
- Proactive audits in high-risk industries
- Responds to worker complaints
- Can prosecute without worker cooperation

High-risk industries:
- Construction
- Transport/logistics
- Cleaning
- Agriculture
- IT contracting

## How to Get It Right

1. Apply the [[contractors/employee-vs-contractor-test|multi-factor test]] honestly
2. Structure genuine contracting arrangements properly
3. Don't convert employees to contractors
4. Get legal/accounting advice for borderline cases
5. Document the genuine business reasons for contractor arrangements
