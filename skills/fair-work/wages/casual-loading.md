# Casual Loading

Casuals receive a 25% loading on top of the base rate instead of paid leave.

## What the Loading Compensates For

The 25% is meant to compensate for NOT getting:
- Paid annual leave (4 weeks)
- Paid personal/sick leave (10 days)
- Paid compassionate leave
- Notice of termination
- Redundancy pay

## Calculation

```
Casual rate = Base rate × 1.25

Example:
Base rate: $24.10/hr
Casual rate: $24.10 × 1.25 = $30.13/hr
```

## What Casuals Still Get

Even without paid leave, casuals get:
- Public holiday penalty rates if they work
- [[leave/compassionate-leave]] (2 days unpaid)
- [[leave/family-domestic-violence]] (10 days paid)
- Unpaid parental leave (if 12+ months)
- [[wages/superannuation]] (same as everyone)

## Casual Conversion

After 12 months of regular and systematic work, casuals can request conversion to permanent.

Employer can only refuse on reasonable business grounds (and must explain in writing).

Small businesses (<15 employees) don't have to offer conversion but must provide information about it.

## The Casual "Double Dipping" Issue

If someone was called casual but worked like a permanent employee, they might:
1. Already have received casual loading
2. Claim they were actually permanent
3. Seek back-pay for leave entitlements

Courts have ruled this can result in double compensation in some cases. But sham arrangements are still penalized.
