# Superannuation

Employers must pay 12% of ordinary time earnings into employee's super fund.

## Current Rate

**12%** of ordinary time earnings (as of July 2025).

Scheduled increases:
- Was 11% until June 2024
- 11.5% from July 2024
- 12% from July 2025

## Who Gets Super

Almost everyone:
- Full-time employees ✓
- Part-time employees ✓
- Casual employees ✓
- Some contractors (if paid mainly for labour) ✓

**No minimum earning threshold** — even $1 of wages requires super.

**No minimum age** — pay super for workers under 18 who work 30+ hrs/week.

## Ordinary Time Earnings

Super is calculated on:
- Base salary/wages
- Commissions
- Shift loadings
- Allowances (some)
- Paid leave
- Bonuses (usually)

NOT included:
- Overtime (unless overtime is part of ordinary hours)
- Expense reimbursements

## Quarterly Deadlines

| Quarter | Period | Due Date |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Q1 | 1 July – 30 Sept | 28 October |
| Q2 | 1 Oct – 31 Dec | 28 January |
| Q3 | 1 Jan – 31 March | 28 April |
| Q4 | 1 April – 30 June | 28 July |

**Late super = Super Guarantee Charge (SGC):**
- The super owed
- Interest (currently 10%)
- $20 admin fee per employee per quarter
- NOT tax deductible

## Choice of Fund

Employees can choose their super fund. If they don't choose, use:
1. Their existing "stapled" fund (from previous jobs)
2. A default fund nominated in your award/agreement
3. A fund you've chosen as your default
