# Notice Periods

Minimum notice required to end employment. A National Employment Standard.

## Minimum Notice (Employer)

| Period of Service | Notice Required |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Up to 1 year | 1 week |
| 1–3 years | 2 weeks |
| 3–5 years | 3 weeks |
| 5+ years | 4 weeks |

**Add 1 week** if the employee:
- Is over 45 years old, AND
- Has 2+ years continuous service

## Employee Notice

- Check employment contract or [[MOCs/awards|award]]
- Usually same as employer notice
- Many awards require 1-2 weeks regardless of tenure
- If no contract/award term: reasonable notice

## Payment in Lieu

Instead of working notice, employer can pay out:
- Full wages for the notice period
- As a lump sum
- On termination date

Employee doesn't have to agree — employer can choose to pay out.

## Working Out Notice

During notice period:
- Employee must continue to work and perform duties
- Employer must continue to pay and provide work
- Leave can be taken (reduces notice period)

## Garden Leave

Employer can direct employee to stay home during notice:
- Still employed, still paid
- Can't start with new employer
- Common for senior/sensitive roles

## Without Notice

### Employer can dismiss without notice for:
- [[termination/summary-dismissal|Serious misconduct]]
- Theft, fraud, violence
- Serious breach of safety
- Serious breach of employment terms

### Employee can resign without notice for:
- Serious safety concerns
- Employer's serious breach
- (But risky — better to give notice)

## Casual Employees

- No minimum notice required either way
- But should give reasonable notice
- 1 hour to 1 shift is common practice
