# Final Pay

Everything owed to an employee when employment ends.

## What Must Be Paid

### Always Owed

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Outstanding wages | Up to and including last day |
| Accrued annual leave | Including leave loading if applicable |
| Accrued long service leave | If eligible under state law |

### Situation-Dependent

| Item | When It's Owed |
|------|----------------|
| Notice period | If not worked out — [[termination/notice-periods]] |
| Redundancy pay | If genuine redundancy and eligible — [[termination/redundancy-pay]] |
| Bonuses/commissions | If earned before termination |
| Accrued RDOs | If applicable under award |

### NOT Paid Out

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Personal/sick leave | Never paid out |
| Unused family leave | Never paid out |

## Calculating Leave Payout

### Annual Leave

```
Accrued hours × base hourly rate = payout

Example:
80 hours accrued × $25/hr = $2,000

Plus leave loading if applicable:
$2,000 × 17.5% = $350 loading
Total: $2,350
```

### Long Service Leave

State-based entitlements. Generally:
- Pro-rata after 7 years (most states)
- Full entitlement after 10 years
- Rate varies by state

## When It's Due

- **Best practice:** On last day of employment
- **Legal requirement:** Within 7 days of termination (most awards)
- **Maximum:** Next regular pay cycle

Late payment can result in:
- Interest
- Penalties
- Small claims/FWC proceedings

## Deductions

Employer can only deduct from final pay if:
- Required by law (tax, court orders)
- Employee has agreed in writing
- Authorized by award/agreement

**Cannot deduct** for:
- Training costs (unless specific agreement)
- Uniform not returned (unless agreed)
- Till shortages
- Damage (generally)

## Disputes

If final pay is wrong or not paid:
1. Raise with employer in writing
2. Contact Fair Work Ombudsman
3. Lodge small claim if needed
4. FWC can deal with underpayment claims
