# Small Business Fair Dismissal Code

Special rules for businesses with fewer than 15 employees.

## What It Provides

If you follow the code, a dismissal will be considered fair — even if employee claims otherwise.

## Who It Applies To

- Businesses with **fewer than 15 employees** at time of dismissal
- Count all employees (including casuals employed on a regular and systematic basis)
- Count employees of associated entities

## The Code Requirements

### For Serious Misconduct (Summary Dismissal)

Can dismiss immediately without warning if employee has committed serious misconduct:
- Theft
- Fraud
- Violence
- Serious safety breaches
- Causing serious and imminent risk

Must have **reasonable belief** the conduct occurred.

### For Other Valid Reasons

Must follow these steps:

#### 1. Valid Reason
The reason for dismissal must be:
- Based on the employee's conduct, or
- Based on their capacity to do the job

#### 2. Warning (Unless Serious Misconduct)

Before dismissing:
- Tell the employee why their job is at risk
- Give them a chance to respond
- **Warning should be in writing** (best practice)
- Keep records

#### 3. Opportunity to Respond

Employee must have chance to:
- Understand the issues
- Respond to allegations
- Have a support person present (if they request)

#### 4. Genuine Belief

Employer must genuinely believe:
- The reason is valid
- Dismissal is warranted
- The process has been fair

## What "Fair" Doesn't Mean

The code doesn't require:
- Multiple written warnings
- Performance improvement plans
- Perfect process

It requires:
- Valid reason
- Opportunity to respond
- Genuine belief dismissal is warranted

## Checklist

Before dismissing:

- [ ] Business has <15 employees?
- [ ] Is conduct serious misconduct? (If yes, can dismiss immediately with reasonable belief)
- [ ] Have you told employee the reason their job is at risk?
- [ ] Have you given them chance to respond?
- [ ] Did you offer a support person?
- [ ] Do you genuinely believe dismissal is warranted?
- [ ] Have you documented the process?

## If You Don't Follow the Code

Employee can claim unfair dismissal if:
- Employed 12+ months (small business = longer qualifying period)
- Dismissal was harsh, unjust or unreasonable
- Code was not followed
