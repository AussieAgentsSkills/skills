# Contractors vs Employees

This is where businesses get in serious trouble. The legal test doesn't care what the contract says.

## Why It Matters

If someone is legally an employee but you've treated them as a contractor:
- You owe back-pay for all underpaid entitlements
- Unpaid superannuation + interest
- Unpaid leave entitlements
- Potential fines for sham contracting
- They can claim unfair dismissal

## The Test

### [[contractors/employee-vs-contractor-test|Employee vs Contractor Test]]

The law looks at the **real nature of the relationship**, not what the contract says.

Key indicators of EMPLOYEE:
- You control how, when, where they work
- They use your tools/equipment
- They're paid by the hour/day, not per job
- They can't subcontract or delegate
- They work exclusively or mainly for you
- They're integrated into your business
- You provide training
- They wear your uniform

Key indicators of CONTRACTOR:
- They control how they complete the work
- They use their own tools/equipment
- They quote per job, invoice for completed work
- They can subcontract or send a replacement
- They work for multiple clients
- They have their own ABN and business structure
- They carry their own insurance
- They can make profit or loss on the job

**No single factor is decisive.** Courts weigh the whole picture.

## Common Mistakes

### [[contractors/sham-contracting|Sham Contracting]]

Calling someone a contractor when they're really an employee. This is illegal.

Examples:
- Existing employee becomes "contractor" doing same job
- "Contractor" but you set their hours and they only work for you
- ABN required as condition of hiring

Penalties: Up to $93,900 per breach (individuals) or $469,500 (companies).

### The Gig Economy Problem

Many "gig workers" have been found to be employees:
- Deliveroo riders
- Uber drivers (in some cases)

If the platform controls enough of the relationship, they're employees.

## How to Get It Right

1. **Genuinely structure the relationship as contracting:**
   - Written contract for specific deliverables
   - They quote and invoice
   - They control how work is done
   - They can work for others
   - They use their own equipment

2. **Don't convert employees to contractors:**
   - Same work = same relationship
   - Redundancy then immediate contractor = red flag

3. **Get advice for borderline cases:**
   - ATO contractor decision tool
   - Fair Work Ombudsman
   - Employment lawyer
