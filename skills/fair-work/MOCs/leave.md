# Leave Entitlements

The National Employment Standards (NES) set minimum leave. Awards and agreements can add more, never less.

## Types of Leave

### [[leave/annual-leave|Annual Leave]]
4 weeks per year for full-time. Accrues progressively. Can be taken when agreed with employer.

### [[leave/personal-leave|Personal/Carer's Leave]]
10 days per year. For when you're sick OR caring for sick family member. Doesn't expire.

### [[leave/compassionate-leave|Compassionate Leave]]
2 days per occasion. Death or serious illness of close family member.

### [[leave/parental-leave|Parental Leave]]
Up to 12 months unpaid (can request additional 12). Plus government Paid Parental Leave scheme.

### [[leave/public-holidays|Public Holidays]]
Paid day off on gazetted public holidays. Penalty rates if you work.

### [[leave/long-service-leave|Long Service Leave]]
State-based. Usually 8.67 weeks after 10 years continuous service.

### [[leave/family-domestic-violence|Family & Domestic Violence Leave]]
10 days paid per year. Confidential.

## Casuals and Leave

Casuals don't get paid annual or personal leave — that's what the 25% [[wages/casual-loading|casual loading]] compensates for.

Casuals DO get:
- 2 days unpaid compassionate leave
- 2 days unpaid family emergency leave
- Unpaid parental leave (if 12+ months service)
- Family & domestic violence leave

## Leave Accrual

Leave accrues from day one of employment:
- Annual: 4 weeks ÷ 52 = 0.077 weeks per week worked
- Personal: 10 days ÷ 52 = 0.19 days per week worked

Part-timers accrue pro-rata based on hours.

## Requesting Leave

1. Employee requests leave
2. Employer can't unreasonably refuse annual leave
3. Employer CAN direct when leave is taken in some circumstances (shutdown periods)
4. Personal leave just needs reasonable notice + evidence if requested

## Cashing Out Leave

Annual leave can be cashed out if:
- Award or agreement allows
- Employee keeps minimum 4 weeks accrued
- Written agreement for each cash-out
- Payment at current rate
