# Awards & Classifications

Modern awards set minimum pay and conditions for industries/occupations. Most employees are covered by one.

## What is a Modern Award?

A legal document that sets minimum:
- Pay rates by classification level
- Penalty rates (weekends, public holidays, overtime)
- Allowances (travel, uniforms, tools)
- Leave entitlements beyond NES
- Rostering and hours rules

There are 121 modern awards covering most industries.

## Am I Covered by an Award?

```
Is the business incorporated (Pty Ltd, Ltd)?
  └── Yes → Federal Fair Work system applies
  
Does an enterprise agreement apply?
  └── Yes → Agreement overrides award (but can't go below NES)
  └── No → Check for relevant award
  
Is there an award for this industry/occupation?
  └── Yes → That award applies
  └── No → Award-free (just NES + contract)
```

## Classification Levels

Each award has classification levels (e.g., Level 1, Level 2...). Higher level = higher pay.

Factors that determine level:
- Skills and qualifications
- Experience
- Supervision responsibilities
- Complexity of work

See individual award pages for specific level definitions.

## Finding Your Award

1. **FWO Award Finder**: https://www.fairwork.gov.au/awards-and-agreements/awards/find-my-award
2. Look at the industry (what the business does)
3. Look at the occupation (what the worker does)
4. Some employees are covered by occupation awards regardless of industry

## Award-Free Employees

Some employees aren't covered by any award:
- Managers earning over high-income threshold ($175,000+)
- Some professionals (lawyers, accountants, doctors in private practice)
- Very small businesses in niche industries

They still get NES minimums: [[leave/annual-leave]], [[termination/notice-periods]], etc.
