# Ending Employment

How employment ends — whether initiated by employer or employee.

## Ways Employment Ends

### [[termination/resignation|Resignation]]
Employee quits. Usually must give notice (check contract/award). Can be immediate in serious situations.

### [[termination/dismissal|Dismissal]]
Employer ends employment. Must follow proper process or risk unfair dismissal claim.

### [[termination/redundancy|Redundancy]]
Job no longer needed. Genuine redundancy has specific requirements. Redundancy pay may apply.

### [[termination/summary-dismissal|Summary Dismissal]]
Immediate dismissal for serious misconduct. No notice. Must be genuinely serious.

### [[termination/end-of-contract|End of Fixed-Term Contract]]
Contract expires. No notice required unless contract says otherwise.

## Notice Periods

### [[termination/notice-periods|Minimum Notice by Tenure]]

| Years of Service | Notice Period |
|------------------|---------------|
| Up to 1 year | 1 week |
| 1-3 years | 2 weeks |
| 3-5 years | 3 weeks |
| 5+ years | 4 weeks |

Add 1 week if employee is over 45 AND has 2+ years service.

Payment in lieu of notice is allowed — pay out the notice period instead of working it.

## Unfair Dismissal

Employee can claim unfair dismissal if:
- Employed 6+ months (or 12 months for small business <15 employees)
- Dismissal was harsh, unjust or unreasonable
- Not genuine redundancy
- Not serious misconduct

Small Business Fair Dismissal Code provides protection if followed correctly — see [[termination/small-business-code]].

## Redundancy Pay

### [[termination/redundancy-pay|Redundancy Pay by Tenure]]

| Years of Service | Redundancy Pay |
|------------------|----------------|
| 1-2 years | 4 weeks |
| 2-3 years | 6 weeks |
| 3-4 years | 7 weeks |
| 4-5 years | 8 weeks |
| 5-6 years | 10 weeks |
| 6-7 years | 11 weeks |
| 7-8 years | 13 weeks |
| 8-9 years | 14 weeks |
| 9-10 years | 16 weeks |
| 10+ years | 12 weeks |

Small businesses (<15 employees) are exempt from redundancy pay.

## Final Pay

### [[termination/final-pay|What Must Be Paid]]

On termination, employee is owed:
- Outstanding wages up to last day
- Accrued annual leave (including loading if applicable)
- Accrued long service leave (if eligible)
- Notice period (if not worked out)
- Redundancy pay (if applicable)

Personal/sick leave is NOT paid out — it's "use it or lose it."

Final pay usually due within 7 days of termination.
