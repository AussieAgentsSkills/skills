# QLD Transfer Duty (Stamp Duty)

You are an expert in Queensland transfer duty for property transactions.

## Standard Duty Rates (2024-25)

| Property Value | Duty Rate |
|----------------|-----------|
| $0 - $5,000 | Nil |
| $5,001 - $75,000 | $1.50 per $100 (over $5,000) |
| $75,001 - $540,000 | $1,050 + $3.50 per $100 (over $75,000) |
| $540,001 - $1,000,000 | $17,325 + $4.50 per $100 (over $540,000) |
| Over $1,000,000 | $38,025 + $5.75 per $100 (over $1,000,000) |

## First Home Buyer Concessions

### First Home Concession
- **Vacant land:** Up to $400,000 (no duty)
- **Home:** Up to $550,000 (no duty)
- Sliding scale to $700,000 (reduced duty)

### First Home Owner Grant
- **$30,000** for new homes (extended to June 2025)
- Must be new or substantially renovated
- Property value under $750,000

### Eligibility
- Australian citizen or permanent resident
- At least 18 years old
- Never owned property in Australia
- Live in property within 1 year
- Live there for at least 6 continuous months

## Home Concession (Non-First Home)

For home buyers (not first home):
- Up to $350,000: Reduced rates apply
- Sliding scale up to $645,000
- Must be principal place of residence

## Foreign Buyer Surcharge

- **Additional 7%** for foreign purchasers
- Applies on top of standard duty
- FIRB approval also required

## Quick Calculations

### Example 1: First home buyer $500,000
- First home concession applies
- **Duty: $0** (under $550,000 threshold)

### Example 2: Investment property $600,000
- $17,325 + ($600,000 - $540,000) × 4.5%
- $17,325 + $2,700 = **$20,025**

### Example 3: $800,000 home (not first home)
- $17,325 + ($800,000 - $540,000) × 4.5%
- $17,325 + $11,700 = **$29,025**

## When Duty is Due

- Within **30 days** of settlement
- Electronic lodgement via OSR
- Late payment incurs penalty tax

## Off-the-Plan Concessions

- Duty on land value only at contract date
- Building component not included
- Must settle within 3 years

## Exemptions

- Transfers between spouses/de factos (main residence)
- Deceased estates to beneficiaries
- Family farm transfers
- Corporate reconstructions (conditions apply)
