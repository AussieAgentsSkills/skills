# Capital Gains Tax (CGT)

You are an expert in Australian Capital Gains Tax.

## When CGT Applies

CGT applies when you dispose of a CGT asset:
- Sell it
- Gift it
- Trade/exchange it
- Lose or destroy it (insurance payout)
- It's compulsorily acquired

## CGT Assets

**Included:**
- Real estate (not main residence)
- Shares and managed funds
- Cryptocurrency
- Collectables (>$500)
- Personal use assets (>$10,000)
- Business assets
- Contracts and leases

**Excluded:**
- Main residence (with conditions)
- Cars and motorcycles
- Depreciating assets used for business
- Assets acquired before 20 Sep 1985

## Calculating CGT

### Basic Formula
```
Capital Gain = Capital Proceeds - Cost Base

Cost Base includes:
- Purchase price
- Incidental costs (stamp duty, legal fees)
- Improvement costs
- Costs of disposal
```

### CGT Discount (Individuals & Trusts)

If held >12 months:
- **Individuals:** 50% discount
- **SMSF:** 33.33% discount
- **Companies:** No discount

### Example
```
Bought shares: $10,000 (2020)
Sold shares: $25,000 (2024)
Capital gain: $15,000
After 50% discount: $7,500
Added to taxable income: $7,500
```

## Main Residence Exemption

**Full exemption if:**
- You live in it as main residence
- Land ≤2 hectares
- Not used to produce income

**Partial exemption if:**
- Rented out part of time
- Used for business
- Land >2 hectares

### 6-Year Absence Rule
Can treat property as main residence for up to 6 years while:
- Living elsewhere
- Renting it out
- Not claiming another main residence

## CGT Events

| Event | When |
|-------|------|
| A1 | Disposal of asset |
| B1 | Use & enjoyment before title passes |
| C1 | Loss/destruction of asset |
| C2 | Cancellation of rights |
| D1 | Creating contractual rights |
| E1 | Trust: beneficiary entitled to asset |
| K3 | Loss from collectables |

## Small Business CGT Concessions

4 concessions for eligible small businesses:
1. **15-year exemption** (held 15+ years, retiring)
2. **50% active asset reduction**
3. **Retirement exemption** (up to $500k lifetime)
4. **Rollover** (defer gain, reinvest in replacement asset)

**Eligibility:**
- Turnover <$2 million OR
- Net CGT assets <$6 million

## Cryptocurrency

Crypto is a CGT asset:
- Each disposal triggers CGT event
- Swapping crypto = disposal
- Staking rewards = ordinary income
- Keep detailed records

## Record Keeping

Keep for 5 years after disposal:
- Purchase contracts/receipts
- Improvement receipts
- Valuations
- Sale contracts
- Cost of acquisition

## CGT on Death

No CGT when assets pass to beneficiary. Beneficiary inherits your cost base:
- If acquired pre-CGT: market value at death
- If acquired post-CGT: deceased's cost base

## Reporting

- Report in tax return (Schedule: Capital gains)
- Offset capital losses against gains
- Carry forward unused losses indefinitely
- Cannot offset against ordinary income
